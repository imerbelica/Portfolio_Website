Space Mission Cost Analysis

    Skills/Technologies Used

  • Python
  • Kaggle(Data Source)

During the Fall 2020 semester at the University of Hartford I was tasked with a partner project in my Data Mining course. The concept of the project was simple, find a topic of interest find a large dataset related to that topic and draw some conclusions on the topic at hand based upon the data presented. We were free to use our choice of technologies, and data sources to accomplish the task and would need to present our finding upon the completion of the task. This project expanded my knowledge of how the data mining and analysis workflow takes place and created more familiarity with the language we chose to accomplish the tasks outlined, this being Python. As a topic we selected space, and we were able to find a dataset on Kaggle describing every global space mission that’s taken place since 1959. We performed a cost analysis on these missions comparing the cost efficiency of private and public space missions and came to the conclusion that generally private space missions tend to be more cost-efficient, upon doing some research we concluded that this was likely due to lightened regulations for the private sector. My comfortability with python grew substantially throughout the project particularly with specific popular libraries offered by the language such as pandas, sklearn, and scipy. The project as a whole really helped me grow as a developer and data scientist.

NASA Rock on Workshop

    Skills/Technologies Used

  • Arduino Microcontroller (C++)
  • Soldering

In June 2021, I took part in one of the most rewarding learning experiences of my college career, the NASA Rock On Workshop. This was a two-week-long workshop in which groups from twenty-five universities from around the country worked together to build space instruments capable of collecting environmental readings 73 miles above the earths surface. The payloads built during the workshop would be sent to space on NASA’s two-stage Terrier-Orion rocket and upon its landing in the Atlantic Ocean the payloads would be retrieved such that the data can be viewed and analyzed. The Experience as a whole was amazing and I was able to develop a number of skills throughout, students were responsible for assembling the payload by soldering the sensors and instruments to the PCBs provided in the kit, programming the Arduino boards using c++, and testing each of the components before flight. I have a real passion for space, because of this I found this entire experience to be really rewarding and enjoyable.

Active Suspension System Simulation

    Skills/Technologies Used

  • NI LabView

During the Fall 2021 semester I took on a course that gave me much more insight into the mechanical engineering discipline, this course was Mechatronics and System Design. This was a project based course that taught the fundamentals of mechatronics, a combination of mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, and computer science. I gained a number of skills throughout the semester like the ability to take a problem statement and derive a free body diagram, block diagram, equation of motion, and eventually Lab View simulation from that problem statement. I utilized these skills during the final project we were tasked with at the end of the semester which was to simulate a real world problem that could be remedied using Mechatronics practices. I chose to model and simulate an active suspension system using a PID controller on Lab View and was able to demonstrate the ability of such a system to maximize both the comfort and handling of the vehicle in question. Additionally, I simulated a passive suspension system in order to contrast the two options and demonstrate the superiority of the active system. The project gave me experience and exposure to the Lab View simulation software, mechanical systems, and the planning and modeling system that takes place when developing engineering solutions.

Scrum Dinger iOS Application

    Skills/Technologies Used

  • Swift

Upon Graduation in December 2021, I grew more interested in development on apple platforms, as a result I thought it would be fitting to take on a swift project, more specifically a swift project that could have a practical application in the workplace. Having made some simple applications prior using swift I felt ready to take the steps necessary to make something fully featured and really polished, I found exactly what I was looking for when I stumbled on Apples first party intermediate course on the swift programming language. This course helped me better understand the language and the features it offered to developers, the course also outlined how to take an application to the App Store from scratch, that application was Scrum Dinger. The concept of the application is simple and incredibly functional, moreover it contains a number of the programming characteristics that most useful applications would require. Scrum Dinger was a tool that could help development teams manage their scrum sessions, by allowing users to customize parameters such as team members, scrum durations, and projects titles and later conduct their scrum meetings according to these parameters. The app would run timers showing each member they’re remaining time and notifying them when that time would be up. While the app is conceptually simple, it is fully featured, which is why it was such a great learning experience, it allowed me to learn things like adding reminders from within an application and adding accessibility functionality to an application to cater to all users.
